Tools

This section introduces the tools and materials used throughout the risograph printing process.

Printing Equipment

Risograph Printer Brands
RISO is the primary manufacturer associated with risograph printing and is widely used in creative and educational settings. Other duplicator brands exist, but RISO machines are preferred for their ink system, artistic flexibility, and strong presence in design communities.

Inks & Stencils

Ink Drums
Ink drums are removable components that hold a single color of ink inside the risograph machine. Each drum corresponds to one color, and printing with multiple colors requires swapping drums and running the paper through the machine multiple times.
Soy-Based Inks
Risograph inks are soy-based and semi-transparent, which allows colors to overlap and blend visually when layered. This translucency contributes to the distinctive color variations and textures commonly seen in risograph prints.
Masters
Masters are thin stencils created from digital artwork and wrapped around the ink drum. They control where ink is applied to the paper, and a new master must be created for each color used in a design. Over time, masters wear out and can affect print consistency.

Materials & Preparation

Uncoated Paper
Uncoated paper is commonly used in risograph printing because it absorbs ink more effectively than coated paper. Paper weight, texture, and absorbency all influence how the ink spreads and how well colors align during multiple print passes.
Digital Design Tools
Digital design tools are used to prepare artwork before printing by separating colors, adjusting contrast, and creating halftones. Proper file preparation is essential to ensure that each color layer prints accurately when transferred onto a master.
