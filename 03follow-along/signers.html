Who Were the Signers

Famous Signers

Some of the most famous signers of the Declaration of Independence include

John Hancock
President of the Continental Congress and best known for his large, bold signature on the Declaration of Independence. He was a major financial supporter of the Revolutionary cause, and his name became synonymous with signing one’s name.
Thomas Jefferson
The primary author of the Declaration of Independence. Jefferson wrote many of its most famous passages, including those concerning natural rights, liberty, and equality, which have had lasting influence on democratic ideals.
Benjamin Franklin
A renowned statesman, inventor, and diplomat. Franklin played a crucial role in securing French support for the American Revolution and was one of the most respected and internationally known figures among the Founding Fathers.
John Adams
A strong advocate for independence and an influential leader in the Continental Congress. Adams later helped negotiate the Treaty of Paris, which officially ended the Revolutionary War, and went on to become the second President of the United States.
Samuel Adams
A revolutionary leader and organizer who helped mobilize colonial resistance to British rule. He was heavily involved in events such as the Boston Tea Party and used political writing and activism to rally public support for independence.
Roger Sherman
A statesman from Connecticut and the only person to sign all four major founding documents of the United States: the Declaration of Independence, the Articles of Confederation, the Constitution, and the Bill of Rights.
Robert Morris
Known as the “Financier of the American Revolution,” Morris used his personal wealth and financial expertise to help fund the Continental Army, especially during times when official funds were scarce.
John Witherspoon
A clergyman and educator who signed the Declaration of Independence. He served as president of the College of New Jersey (now Princeton University) and educated many future American political leaders.
Charles Carroll of Carrollton
The wealthiest signer of the Declaration and the only Catholic among them. He signed his full name to clearly identify himself, fully aware of the personal risk involved in declaring independence.

Each of these individuals played a significant role in the founding of the United States and is remembered for their courage in signing the Declaration, an act considered treasonous by the British Crown.
